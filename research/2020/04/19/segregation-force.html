Granular segregation: from particle-scale physics to continuum modeling

Apr 19, 2020

This research aims to advance fundamental understanding of granular segregation at the particle level and enhance predictive modeling of size and/or density segregation in various flow configurations.

The first result is how large and small particles migrate at the particle scale, hinting at completely different physics (Jing et al., 2017).

Now at Northwestern, the force driving size and density segregation at the single intruder limit is being characterized using a virtual spring-based force meter in numerical simulations. A simple scaling law is proposed, which enables the first a priori prediction of rising and sinking of large intruders in free surface granular flows (Jing et al., 2020).

  1. Jing, L., Kwok, C. Y., & Leung, Y. F. (2017). Micromechanical origin of particle size segregation. Physical Review Letters, 118(11), 118001. https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevLett.118.118001
  2. Jing, L., Ottino, J. M., Lueptow, R. M., & Umbanhowar, P. B. (2020). Rising and sinking intruders in dense granular flows. Submitted.
